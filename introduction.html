Hey Foxy Program Privacy Notice

 

November 1, 2017

 

The Mozilla Privacy Policy describes how we handle information that we receive from you in connection with your participation in the Hey Foxy Program (“Hey Foxy”).

Things you should know

The Hey Foxy Program is an experimental program and is subject to change at any time. Mozilla will make reasonable efforts to keep you updated and informed of any significant Program changes; however, given the experimental nature of both the Program and the services provided under the Program, you acknowledge and accept any changes that may occur during the Program.  By participating in the Program, you are agreeing to take part in an experimental use of the Hey Foxy voice assistant technology.

 

Mozilla receives data when Hey Foxy is started, stopped and when the user uses the wakeword (“Hey Foxy”). We use this data to build better Hey Foxy features.

●      General Data Collection: Mozilla receives data from Hey Foxy when the Program is started, stopped and when the Hey Foxy wakeword is used. The following user data is collected:

 

○      Telemetry and Browser Data: You may have configured your Firefox browser to allow Telemetry to send Mozilla data about your browser use, which could include your use of the Hey Foxy Program. Telemetry is a feature in Firefox that sends Mozilla usage, performance, and responsiveness statistics about user interface features, memory, and hardware configuration.  You can learn more about the Telemetry data Mozilla gathers by reading the Firefox Privacy Notice.  Your IP address is also collected as a part of a standard server log.

 

○      Usage Data: In addition to the normal data sent by Firefox’s Telemetry, Hey Foxy also sends Mozilla information about your use of Hey Foxy. For example, Mozilla receives your voice input so that it can convert it to text, which is then saved on its servers.  Specifically, the Program collects the text of words spoken after you use the “Hey Foxy” wakeword. Your voice input is processed through the Program and several third party services (discussed below).  Mozilla receives usage data when Hey Foxy starts or stops, when any errors occur, or when you use your voice to ask it to do something (e.g., ask for the weather or request music to be streamed). We collect this data so we can better understand how users interact with Hey Foxy.

 

●      Third Party Data Collection: Mozilla uses several third-party services as integrations (the “Integrations”) in the Hey Foxy Program. The following services – and their specific function – are used in the Program:

 

○      Music Streaming. The Hey Foxy Program allows the user to stream music by voice command through the browser. Mozilla uses Spotify as the music streaming service. Spotify collects certain user data as part of the provision of its service, so be sure to review both their terms and privacy policy found at https://developer.spotify.com/technologies/widgets/terms-of-use/ and (https://www.spotify.com/us/legal/privacy-policy/, respectively.

 

○      Weather. The Hey Foxy Program allows the user to access global weather data by voice command. Mozilla uses openweathermap.org as the provider of weather data. Openweathermap.org collects certain user data when it provides its service, so be sure to review their terms and privacy policy found at http://openweathermap.org/terms and http://openweathermap.org/privacy-policy, respectively.

 

○      Hotword Detection. The Hey Foxy Program requires a third-party engine to detect the “Hey Foxy” hotword spoken by users to give commands. Mozilla uses the Snowboy detection engine (https://snowboy.kitt.ai/) for this function. Snowboy’s maker, KITT.AI, may collect certain user data, so be sure to review their terms of service and privacy policy here: http://kitt.ai/legal/

 

○      Voice Processing. The Hey Foxy Program uses voice- and language-processing software as part of its service to the user. The maker of this software, DialogFlow, may collect user data, so be sure to read their terms of service and privacy policy here: https://dialogflow.com/terms-and-privacy

●      Server Logs: Your interaction with the Hey Foxy extension, including its installation, is collected as part of a standard server log. This server log data will be associated with your IP address, and will be routinely destroyed in accordance with our Privacy Policies.

 

●      Communications: While you are participating in the Hey Foxy Program, we may use your email address to ask for feedback and notify you of any important Hey Foxy updates.

 

●      Analytics & Non-Mozilla Services: We use services of other companies like BigQuery and Google for analytics. We encourage you to review their privacy policies and terms, as they are different from ours.
